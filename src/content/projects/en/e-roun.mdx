## The problem it started from

Most child cognitive-training results survive only as "right or wrong." But to design the next session, a counselor needs **how the child solved it** — response speed, focus, whether they changed their answer. E-roun is an integrated service where children train through play on a tablet while counselors review those records as growth data on a web dashboard.

## What I did

### Training that feels like play, not a test

In the children's app I built game-style training screens across attention, memory, language, and problem-solving. I designed the flow between sessions and a stamp-reward structure so it never feels like being examined.

### A dashboard counselors can actually use

On the counselor web I built screens for the child roster, training schedules, per-question results (chosen answer · correct answer · focus · response speed), cumulative statistics, and per-domain progress. **Separating single-session analysis from cumulative analysis** lets counselors handle today's session and long-term growth differently.

### Applying real-world feedback

Eight children used the service and counselors left 19 comments, which drove improvements to screen flow and how results are reviewed. I learned how a builder's assumptions differ from how the field actually works, presented that process, and the project won an Excellence Award in SSAFY's capstone program.

## Where this meets HR practice

Recording human activity as data and using it to design the next stage of growth is **the same skeleton as HRD's training operations and effectiveness reviews**. And positioning the AI report as "a tool that supports the counselor's judgment" rather than "a replacement for it" is exactly how I intend to use AI in HR.
