## The problem it started from

When trade in one item rises, some items follow months later. The competition asked us to find co-movement between items in trade data and forecast future transaction amounts — and I was responsible for building the AI model.

## What I did

### Time-series preprocessing

I aggregated monthly transaction amounts per item and converted them into year-month time series, along with criteria for filtering out items whose trading history was too thin to trust.

### Lagged correlation analysis

To find leading–lagging relationships between items, I ran correlation analysis with lags of up to 8 months and set a correlation-coefficient threshold, identifying 3,539 significant co-movement candidate pairs.

### Forecasting model

I forecasted future transaction amounts per item with a LightGBM regression model and combined the predictions with the co-movement candidates for the final submission, reaching the top 16.5% of the DACON leaderboard.

## Where this meets HR practice

I carried out the full data-analysis cycle myself — preprocessing, setting analysis criteria, modeling, and packaging results. It became my **foundation for handling HR data**, from attendance-pattern analysis to workforce-demand forecasting.
